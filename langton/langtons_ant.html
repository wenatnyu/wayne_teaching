About Langton's Ant

Langton's Ant is a two-dimensional cellular automaton that follows very simple rules but creates surprisingly complex patterns. Despite the simplicity of the rules, the ant creates a seemingly random pattern for about 10,000 steps, then starts building a repeating "highway" pattern.

This demonstrates how complex behavior can emerge from simple rules - a key concept in complexity theory and artificial life.
